i

i an imaginary number used to represent the square root of -1. It's introduction as a concept in mathematics has enabled solutions for many problems that were previously thought to be unsolvable. For example, the equation x^2 + 1 can initally appear to have no solutions when the function is plotted on a graph since the parabola is always above the x axis. However, this is contradictory to our basic understanding of polynomials and algebra which states that an equation with terms to the nth pwoer will have exactly n roots (solutions).

By rearranging the equation to equal zero and taking roots of both sides we are left with w = the square root of -1 and the negative square root of -1. This solution was given the letter i (+/-).
