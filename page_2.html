e (Euler's Number)

e is an mathematical constant, similar to pi in that it is irrational and also has a wide range of applications across many fields and subject areas. Although e can be found as the sum to infinty of the series 1/n! it is also the only value of a such that the graph of y = a^x has a gradient function of a^x i.e. if y = e^x, dy/dx = e^x.

Another use of e is in the integral of the function f(x) = 1/x. By integrating this function with limits of 1 and e respectively the resulting absolute integral is 1. This is because the intergral of 1/x is ln(x) (log to base e). As such, the resulting calculation is ln(e) - ln(1) or 1 - 0 = 1.
